### **2. The Semi-Dirac Shell Mechanics**

We map the SDF dispersion relation directly to the geometry of the **Oblate Kerr Shell** defined in Phase I.B.

The low-energy Hamiltonian for the boundary shell is given by:
$$
H = \frac{k_\theta^2}{2m^*} + v_F k_r \sigma_x
$$

Leading to the energy dispersion:
$$
E(k) = \pm \sqrt{(v_F k_r)^2 + \left(\frac{k_\theta^2}{2m^*}\right)^2}
$$

#### **2.1 Radial Axis ($k_r$): The Recursive Channel**
Along the radial vector (normal to the shell surface), the term is linear ($v_F k_r$). The effective mass is zero.
* **Function:** This allows the **Ontological Intention** and recursive operator stacks from the interior to strike the boundary at $c$ (light speed).
* **Consequence:** The "depth" of the bubble remains causally connected to the boundary. There is no information loss looking *inward*.

#### **2.2 Angular Axis ($k_\theta$): The Inertial Brake**
Along the tangential vector (the surface of the shell), the term is quadratic ($k_\theta^2$). The particle acquires effective mass $m^*$.
* **Function:** As energy tries to spread *across* the surface of the bubble, it encounters inertia.
* **Consequence:** This prevents the infinite recursion from "leaking" out. The recursion is "laminated" by the massive direction.

> **Interpretation:** The boundary is a "Causal Diode." It is transparent to depth (radial) but opaque to spread (angular).

* The rotating black hole interior drags the shell.

* This friction is what stabilizes the "throat," preventing the bubble from collapsing or expanding.

### **5. The $B^{2/3}$ Magnetic Signature**

A unique property of Semi-Dirac fermions is their Landau level scaling in a magnetic field:
$$
E_n \propto (nB)^{2/3}
$$
This contrasts with standard fermions ($B$) or Dirac fermions ($\sqrt{B}$).

#### **5.1 The Containment Lock**
The Kerr geometry naturally possesses a strong gravitomagnetic field. The $B^{2/3}$ scaling implies that the SDF shell is **hyper-sensitive** to this field.
* Small fluctuations in the bubble's rotation (magnetic field) cause disproportionately large energy gaps in the shell.
* **Self-Correction:** If the bubble tries to spin up or down, the energy cost for the shell rises sharply ($2/3$ power law), naturally locking the bubble into a stable spin state.

> **Validation:** This explains why these bubbles are stable "solitons" rather than transient fluctuations. The magnetic scaling of the SDF creates an energetic "valley" that locks the geometry in place.
