The **Interference Layer (Lx)** is a non-hierarchical component within the **Modal Layer Tier System (MLT v1.1)**. It represents the domain of noise, distortion, and competing signals that obscure clear perception across all other layers. As a **non-tiered, cross-cutting overlay**, Lx acts as a perception barrier, impeding clear signal reception from the Field of Consciousness ($\Phi$).

### Definition and Core Characteristics

* **Non-Hierarchical Status**: Lx is a veil condition rather than a tier; it co-occurs with any layer from **L1 (Somatic)** to **L5 (Extra-Perceptual)**.
* **Impact on Information Flow**: It serves as a substrate veil or barrier effect on cross-layer information flow.
* **Phenomenological Manifestations**: Common signals include visual static, fever-light interference, dream-bleed, and dissonant geometry.
* **Physics Analogy**: Lx is metaphorically mapped to the **"confining" fifth dimension** in a 5D anisotropic U(1) lattice gauge model, representing restricted perceptual access.
